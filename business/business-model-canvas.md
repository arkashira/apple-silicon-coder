## Customer Segments
- Developers and software engineers using Apple Silicon hardware.
- Tech startups focusing on performance-intensive applications.
- Educational institutions teaching programming on Apple Silicon devices.
- Enterprises looking to optimize their software for Apple Silicon architecture.

## Value Propositions
- High-performance coding model optimized for Apple Silicon, achieving at least 200t/s throughput.
- Affordable pricing compared to existing high-performance coding solutions.
- Enhanced reasoning capabilities for better code generation and debugging.
- Seamless integration with existing development environments and tools.

## Channels
- Direct sales through the official website and GitHub repository.
- Partnerships with online coding platforms and educational institutions.
- Marketing through tech blogs, forums, and social media targeting Apple developers.
- Participation in tech conferences and developer meetups to showcase the product.

## Customer Relationships
- Community-driven support via forums and GitHub issues.
- Regular updates and feature releases based on user feedback.
- Dedicated customer support for enterprise clients.
- Educational resources, including tutorials and documentation, to assist users.

- Subscription model: $29/month (approx. 1,000 THB) for individual developers.
- Enterprise licensing: $499/year (approx. 17,500 THB) for businesses.
- One-time purchase option for educational institutions at $199 (approx. 7,000 THB).
- Potential revenue from premium features or add-ons in the future.

- Development team with expertise in AI and Apple Silicon architecture.
- Access to high-performance computing resources for testing and optimization.
- Strong GitHub presence for community engagement and collaboration.
- Marketing and sales team to drive customer acquisition.

## Key Activities
- Continuous development and optimization of the coding model.
- User feedback collection and analysis for product improvement.
- Marketing campaigns to raise awareness and attract customers.
- Building partnerships with educational institutions and tech companies.

## Key Partners
- Apple for potential collaboration and support.
- Educational institutions for promoting the product in their curriculum.
- Online coding platforms for distribution and visibility.
- Influencers and tech bloggers to enhance marketing efforts.

- Development costs: salaries for engineers and researchers.
